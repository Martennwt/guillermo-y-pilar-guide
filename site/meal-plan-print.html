The 45-Day Meal Plan

Everything you eat for the next 45 days: high in protein, easy to keep, and built to lose fat while you hold on to your muscle. Real food, a short menu, and two portion sizes made for each of you.

Amorebieta · Euskal Herria

Read this through slowly and do not skip a page: it is short on purpose, and every part fits together, so read it once from start to finish to understand it all. I have kept it as simple as I possibly can, and you still get three versions of every meal, so the day you are fed up with one, you just switch to another.

Two ways to run this

Path 1: the strict 45-day plan (the challenge). For the next 45 days you follow the plan tightly: hit your calorie target most days, keep the protein high, and take only the one free meal every 2 weeks. This is where the real change happens.

Path 2: the relaxed way. The exact same menu, just looser: aim for the target most days, allow a few more free meals, do not stress a slip. Slower, but very easy to live with. Perfect for after the 45 days, or if strict ever feels like too much.

A strict plan is NOT a boring one. Even on the strict path you get a small menu: 3 breakfasts, 3 lunches, 3 dinners. Each day you pick one from each, in any order. Do not fancy one today? Swap it. The structure is fixed, the choice is yours. Every meal shows two portion sizes: one for Pilar, a bigger one for Guillermo.

The plan is only half of it: move

Eating gets the weight off. What you do with your body decides what KIND of weight comes off. The strongest, best-proven result comes from three things together:

  1. This plan (the calorie deficit, protein high). Drives the fat loss.
  2. A little strength training (push-ups, a kettlebell, simple bodyweight work 2 to 3 times a week). This keeps and even builds muscle while you lose fat, so you end up leaner and stronger, not just lighter.
  3. Regular walks. Extra burn with almost no downside, and they help your head as much as your waist.

Diet alone shrinks you. Diet plus a bit of strength plus walks reshapes you.

Read this first: the rules that make it work

  1. Protein leads the plate. When you eat below your calories, your body can burn fat OR muscle. Protein tells it to burn fat and keep the muscle, so you get leaner and stronger, not just lighter. Daily target: Guillermo about 160 g, Pilar about 110 g. That is why chicken, fish, eggs and egg whites take the big part of the plate, and rice or pasta become the small side.
  2. Your calories a day. Aim inside your band. The strict plan aims at the low end, the relaxed way sits at the high end.
    PersonYour daily bandStrict aims atNever go below
    Guillermo2,200 to 2,400 kcal~2,200 kcal1,600 kcal
    Pilar1,300 to 1,500 kcal~1,300 kcal1,200 kcal
    Do not cut harder than about 500 kcal below your normal burn: past that the body burns muscle and your energy for the site tanks. Moderate cut + protein + a bit of training beats starving, every time.
  3. Two portion sizes, one kitchen. Pilar and Guillermo eat the same dishes. Guillermo just gets a bigger plate, because a man on a building site burns close to 900 kcal a day more. Cook once, serve two sizes. The grams below are a start, not a law.
  4. Eat to "not hungry", not to "stuffed". Your gut tells your brain it is full through hormones (CCK, PYY), and that signal arrives late: about 15 to 20 minutes after you start. So the fix is simple and proven: a glass of water before the meal, eat slowly, then wait. In one trial, water before meals meant 4.3 kg more weight loss over 12 weeks.
  5. Vegetables are free, and required at lunch and dinner. Mostly water: big volume, almost no calories. You never weigh the vegetables.
  6. Water 2 to 3 litres a day. Weigh 3 times a week (morning, after the toilet, before eating) and trust only the weekly average, not a single day.
  7. One free meal every 2 weeks. Not a free day, a free meal. Enjoy it fully, then the next meal you are back on plan. No "making up for it".
  8. Guillermo, the weekend is the trap. Off the scaffolding you burn far less, so keep the base plan and skip the workday extras. Same food that is fine Monday is a surplus Sunday.

Optional, nice to have, not required: a daily omega-3, vitamin D in winter, 5 g creatine if you train, magnesium at night. The plan works without any of them.

L3 · Basque or Mediterranean classic, lightened So it never feels like a diet. Pick any one, cooked lighter (measured oil, more vegetables).

  • Grilled hake or cod + big salad + half a cup of white beans
  • Marmitako (tuna and potato stew), lighter on the oil, heavier on the vegetables

Work-box tip (for the site): any lunch can go cold in a box. Easiest is a big protein salad: tuna or leftover chicken + 2 boiled eggs + a handful of chickpeas over a big salad with a spoon of oil and vinegar. Pilar ~360 kcal / 34 g P, Guillermo ~620 kcal / 58 g P.

Snack: pick 1 (optional)

Mostly for Guillermo on a hard work day (mid-morning or before the site). Pilar usually does not need it: have it only if you moved a lot, or in place of dinner if you trained late.

  • Protein yogurt + a few berries + a few nuts (~180 to 260 kcal, 15 to 22 g protein)
  • Kefir + blueberries + a spoon of oats (~200 kcal, good energy before the site)
  • Cottage cheese + a piece of fruit (~200 kcal, 20 g protein)

Allowed swaps (do not like it? swap it, do not skip the meal)

  • Protein: chicken = turkey = rabbit = white fish = lean pork loin = lean beef = eggs = egg whites = blue fish (sardines, mackerel, a little higher in fat).
  • Carbs: sweet potato = potato = rice = quinoa = legumes. Prefer sweet potato and legumes over white rice and pasta most days.
  • Vegetables: free, and NOT only green. Broccoli, cauliflower, peppers, courgette, green beans, mushrooms, whatever you fancy. Cook them any way you like (oven, pan, boiled, puree).
  • Fats: olive oil = a few nuts = a little avocado = olives. Always measured: a spoon of oil is about 120 kcal.
  • Weigh rice and pasta cooked, everything else raw. Never weigh vegetables.
A sample day, so you see it lands right:

Pilar, steady day: B1 oats jar (400) + L1 oven chicken (470) + D1 burger (300) + small yogurt snack (180) = about 1,350 kcal and 109 g protein. On target.

Guillermo, work day: B1 oats jar (600) + L1 oven chicken (640) + D1 burger (470) + a snack (250) = about 1,960 kcal and 152 g protein. Add a slice of bread or an extra spoon of rice to land around 2,200 to 2,300, inside your band. On target.

The weekly shopping list (2 people, small portions)

Roughly one week for the two of you, sized small on purpose. Buy it, rotate the meals, adjust next week based on what ran out.

Protein (the main spend)

  • Eggs: 1 dozen, plus 3 cartons of egg whites (claras)
  • Chicken: 1.5 to 2 kg (breakfast + lunch)
  • Euskal Okela lean beef (1 to 2 meals): 600 to 800 g
  • White fish or salmon: 800 g to 1 kg
  • Tuna in water: 3 to 4 tins
  • Ham cubes (serrano or turkey): 200 g
  • High-protein yogurt: 1.5 kg; kefir or cottage cheese if you like

Smart carbs (small amounts)

  • Sweet potato: 1.5 to 2 kg
  • Potato: 1 kg
  • Oats: 500 g (lasts ages)
  • Rice: 500 g, or a small bag of quinoa
  • Legumes: 2 tins or bags (chickpeas, lentils)
  • Wholegrain bread: 1 small loaf (Guillermo, work days)

Fruit and fats

  • Blueberries or other berries: 500 g (frozen is fine)
  • Walnuts / almonds: 200 g
  • Extra virgin olive oil, a couple of avocados

Vegetables (free, buy plenty)

  • Spinach, peppers, courgette, onion, tomato, salad, cucumber, green beans, cauliflower, garlic, lemon. Whatever is fresh and cheap that week.

Optional, only if you want them: creatine, omega-3, vitamin D, magnesium.

What 45 days looks like

You are not counting to 45 recipes, you are repeating a good week about 6 times. Tick a box each day you stayed on the plan (a free meal is still a "yes"). Realistic outlook, chase the trend not miracles: Guillermo about 3 to 5 kg (a strong start toward the 15 kg, a 3 to 5 month job); Pilar about 2 to 3 kg, well on the way to the ~6 kg.
