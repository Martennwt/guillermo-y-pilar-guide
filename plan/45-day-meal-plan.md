# The 45-Day Meal Plan: Guillermo & Pilar

*A simple, high-protein eating plan you can actually keep. Not a diet you suffer through: a short menu
of meals you rotate, built so the fat comes off while your muscle stays (and grows once you train).*

## Two ways to run this

**Path 1: the strict 45-day plan (the challenge).** For the next 45 days you follow the plan tightly:
hit your calorie target most days, keep the protein high, and take only the one free meal every 2
weeks. This is the version I built these 45 days for, and it is where the real change happens.

**Path 2: the relaxed way.** The exact same menu, just looser: aim for the target most days, allow a
few more free meals, do not stress a slip. Slower, but very easy to live with. Perfect for after the
45 days, or if the strict path ever feels like too much.

Same food either way. The only difference is how tightly you hold the line.

> **A strict plan is NOT a boring one.** Even on the strict path you are not eating the same meal 45
> times. You get a small menu: **3 breakfasts, 3 lunches, 3 dinners.** Each day you pick one from
> each, in any order. Do not fancy one today? Swap it for another. The structure is fixed, the choice
> is yours. Every meal shows two portion sizes: one for Pilar, a bigger one for Guillermo. That is the
> whole trick.

## The plan is only half of it: move

Eating gets the weight off. What you do with your body decides what KIND of weight comes off. The
strongest, best-proven result comes from three things together:

1. **This plan** (the calorie deficit, protein high). This drives the fat loss.
2. **A little strength training** (even push-ups, a kettlebell, simple bodyweight work 2 to 3 times a
   week). This is the one that keeps and even builds muscle while you lose fat, so you end up leaner
   and stronger, not just lighter. In a review of 25 trials, adding strength training to a diet kept
   far more muscle than dieting alone, or dieting plus only walking.
3. **Regular walks.** Extra calorie burn with almost no downside, and they help your head as much as
   your waist.

Diet alone shrinks you. Diet plus a bit of strength plus walks reshapes you. (See the "Move Your
Body" part of the guide for the simple starting routine.)

## 1. Read this first (the rules that make it work)

**1. Protein leads the plate.** This is the most important line in the whole plan. When you eat below
your calories, your body can burn fat OR muscle for the missing energy. Protein tells it to burn fat
and keep the muscle. In one study, people eating high protein in a deficit lost almost no muscle (0.3
kg) while the low-protein group lost 1.6 kg of muscle for the same weight drop. So you do not just get
lighter, you get leaner and stronger. Your daily protein target:

- **Guillermo: about 160 g of protein a day.**
- **Pilar: about 110 g of protein a day.**

That is why chicken, fish, eggs and egg whites take the big part of the plate, and rice or pasta
become the small side (or disappear some days).

**2. Your calories a day.** I ran your real numbers (weight, height, age, how you move). Aim inside
your band: the strict 45-day plan aims at the low end, the relaxed way sits at the high end.

| | Your daily band | Strict aims at | Never go below |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Guillermo** | 2,200 to 2,400 kcal | ~2,200 kcal | 1,600 kcal |
| **Pilar** | 1,300 to 1,500 kcal | ~1,300 kcal | 1,200 kcal |

Do not eat under the floor, and do not try to cut harder than about 500 kcal below your normal burn.
Past that your body starts burning muscle instead of fat and your energy for the site tanks, which
backfires. A moderate cut plus high protein plus a bit of training beats starving, every single time.

**3. Two portion sizes, one kitchen.** Pilar and Guillermo eat the SAME dishes. Guillermo just gets a
bigger plate, because a man on a building site burns close to 900 kcal a day more than Pilar at home.
Cook once, serve two sizes. The grams below are a starting point, not a law.

**4. Eat to "not hungry", not to "stuffed".** Your gut tells your brain it is full through hormones
(CCK, PYY), and that signal arrives late: about 15 to 20 minutes after you start eating. So if you eat
until you actually feel full, you have already eaten too much. The fix is almost stupidly simple, and
it is proven: **drink a glass of water before the meal, eat slowly, then wait.** In one trial, a glass
of water before meals led to 4.3 kg more weight loss over 12 weeks and people ate 13 to 22% less.
Water first. Slow down. Wait.

**5. Vegetables are free, and required.** At lunch and dinner there must be vegetables, as much as you
want. They are mostly water: big volume, almost no calories, and they fill the plate so the protein
looks generous. You never weigh the vegetables.

**6. Drink 2 to 3 litres of water a day. Weigh 3 times a week** (morning, after the toilet, before
eating) and only trust the weekly average, not a single day. Water weight jumps around; the trend is
what matters.

**7. One free meal every 2 weeks.** Not a free day, a free meal. Enjoy it fully, no guilt, then the
very next meal you are back on plan. No "making up for it" the next day.

**8. Guillermo, the weekend is the trap.** On a work day you burn a huge amount, so you eat the full
plate plus a snack. On Saturday and Sunday off the scaffolding you burn far less, so keep the base
plan and skip the workday extras. Same food that is "fine" Monday is a surplus Sunday.

*(Optional, nice to have, not required: a daily omega-3, vitamin D in winter, 5 g creatine if you
start training, and magnesium at night. No special brand needed, the plan works without any of them.)*

## 2. Breakfast: pick 1 (the make-ahead heroes)

Two of these live in jars in the fridge. On a work morning Guillermo opens the fridge, grabs a jar,
and leaves. No cooking, no thinking. Prep 4 to 5 at once on Sunday.

### B1. Overnight oats and yogurt jar  *(sweet, no cooking, keeps about 4 days)*
Layer oats + high-protein yogurt in a jar the night before. In the morning add the fresh berries and
nuts on top. A pinch of cinnamon.

| | Pilar | Guillermo |
|---|---|---|
| Oats | 45 g | 70 g |
| High-protein yogurt | 175 g | 250 g |
| Blueberries | 80 g | 100 g |
| Walnuts | 12 g | 20 g |
| **Per serving** | **~400 kcal / 26 g protein** | **~600 kcal / 37 g protein** |

### B2. Egg-white omelette jar  *(pure egg whites, no yolk: the lean savory one, keeps about 5 days)*
Beat the egg whites, pour over spinach and onion softened in a little olive oil, add ham cubes. Make
several, keep in the fridge, toppings separate. Guillermo eats it cold or warms it 40 seconds and adds
a spoon of rice for the work day. Want it more filling? Add a slice of wholegrain toast.

| | Pilar | Guillermo |
|---|---|---|
| Egg whites (claras) | 200 g | 300 g |
| Ham cubes (serrano or turkey) | 30 g | 40 g |
| Spinach + onion | free | free |
| Olive oil | 5 g | 8 g |
| Cooked rice | none | 80 g |
| **Per serving** | **~230 kcal / 31 g protein** | **~450 kcal / 47 g protein** |

### B3. Sweet potato and chicken hash  *(warm and savory, a different protein from the egg breakfasts)*
Pan-cook sweet potato cubes, push aside, add spinach and the cooked chicken, warm it through. Batch the
chicken on Sunday. Prefer egg? Swap the chicken for 100 to 150 g egg whites (the sweet-potato-and-egg
version Guillermo asked about).

| | Pilar | Guillermo |
|---|---|---|
| Cooked chicken (or turkey) | 100 g | 160 g |
| Sweet potato | 120 g | 180 g |
| Spinach | free | free |
| Olive oil | 3 g | 5 g |
| **Per serving** | **~300 kcal / 32 g protein** | **~460 kcal / 50 g protein** |

## 3. Lunch: pick 1 (the anchor meal)

Lunch is the biggest meal, especially on a work day. The build is always the same: **a big piece of
protein, a mountain of vegetables, and a small portion of smart carbs.** Protein takes the biggest
part of the plate.

### L1. Oven chicken + sweet potato + veg  *(the batch hero, cook once for 2 to 3 days)*
Throw a tray of chicken, sweet potato and vegetables (peppers, courgette, onion) in the oven with
herbs and a measured spoon of oil. Roast about 30 minutes. Cook a big tray, it covers the next lunches.

| | Pilar | Guillermo |
|---|---|---|
| Chicken (breast or thigh) | 150 g | 220 g |
| Sweet potato | 130 g | 180 g |
| Vegetables | free | free |
| Olive oil | 6 g | 10 g |
| **Per serving** | **~470 kcal / 46 g protein** | **~640 kcal / 68 g protein** |

### L2. Euskal Okela lean beef  *(1 to 2 times a week only)*
Good grass-fed Basque beef (look for the Eusko Label / Euskal Okela at the butcher: born and raised
here, grass-fed, zero-km). Grill a lean steak or make patties, pile on grilled vegetables, a small
potato on the side. Red meat stays a treat, once or twice a week.

| | Pilar | Guillermo |
|---|---|---|
| Lean beef | 110 g | 180 g |
| Grilled vegetables | free | free |
| Potato | 80 g | 120 g |
| Olive oil | 5 g | 8 g |
| **Per serving** | **~380 kcal / 34 g protein** | **~600 kcal / 52 g protein** |

### L3. Basque or Mediterranean classic, lightened  *(so it never feels like a diet)*
Rotate the food you already love, cooked lighter (measured oil, more vegetables). Any one of:
- Grilled hake or cod + big salad + half a cup of white beans
- Marmitako (tuna and potato stew), lighter on the oil, heavier on the vegetables
- Garbanzos con espinacas (chickpeas and spinach) with a piece of cod on top
- Lentejas (lentil stew) with an egg or a little fish for extra protein

Portion so protein leads. **Pilar about 450 kcal, Guillermo about 600 kcal**, protein 30 to 40 g.

> **Work-box tip (for the site):** any lunch can go cold in a box. The easiest is a big protein
> salad: tuna or leftover chicken + 2 boiled eggs + a handful of chickpeas over a big salad with a
> spoon of oil and vinegar. Pilar ~360 kcal / 34 g protein, Guillermo ~620 kcal / 58 g protein.

## 4. Dinner: pick 1 (keep it light)

Dinner is lighter, especially on a low-movement evening: protein plus vegetables, little or no starch,
finished about 3 hours before bed.

### D1. Euskal Okela burger + veg  *(the one Guillermo wanted)*
A good lean grass-fed patty (Euskal Okela), no bun, a big pile of vegetables, and only a small spoon
of rice if you want it. Protein first, carbs tiny.

| | Pilar | Guillermo |
|---|---|---|
| Lean beef patty | 90 g | 150 g |
| Vegetables | free | free |
| Cooked rice (optional) | 40 g | 60 g |
| Olive oil | 3 g | 5 g |
| **Per serving** | **~300 kcal / 22 g protein** | **~470 kcal / 35 g protein** |

### D2. Tortilla ligera  *(light omelette, 5 minutes)*
Mostly egg whites plus one whole egg, ham cubes, and whatever vegetables need using up.

| | Pilar | Guillermo |

Hake, cod or salmon, seared with garlic and lemon, spinach or chard wilted alongside. Light, fast,

Guillermo, take this on a hard work day (mid-morning or before the site). Pilar, you usually do not
need it: have it only if you moved a lot, or use it in place of dinner if you trained late.

- **Protein yogurt + a few berries + a few nuts** (~180 to 260 kcal, 15 to 22 g protein)
- **Kefir + blueberries + a spoon of oats** (~200 kcal, good energy before the site)
- **Cottage cheese + a piece of fruit** (~200 kcal, 20 g protein)

## 6. Allowed swaps (don't like it? swap it, don't skip the meal)

Stay in the same family and the numbers barely move:

- **Protein swaps:** chicken = turkey = rabbit = white fish = lean pork loin = lean beef = eggs =
  egg whites = blue fish (sardines, mackerel, a little higher in fat).
- **Carb swaps:** sweet potato = potato = rice = quinoa = legumes. (Prefer sweet potato and legumes
  over white rice and pasta most days.)
- **Vegetable swaps:** free, and NOT only green. Broccoli, cauliflower, peppers, courgette, green
  beans, mushrooms, whatever you fancy. Cook them any way you like (oven, pan, boiled, puree).
- **Fat swaps:** olive oil = a few nuts = a little avocado = olives. Always measured: a spoon of oil
  is about 120 kcal.
- Weigh rice and pasta **cooked**; weigh everything else **raw**. You never weigh vegetables.

snack (180) = **about 1,350 kcal and 109 g protein.** On target.

**Guillermo, work day:** B1 oats jar (600) + L1 oven chicken (640) + D1 burger (470) + a snack (250)
= **about 1,960 kcal and 152 g protein**. Add a slice of bread or an extra spoon of rice to land
around 2,200 to 2,300, inside your band. On target.

You never have to hit it perfectly. Aim, weigh weekly, adjust.

## 9. The weekly shopping list (2 people, small portions)

This is roughly one week for the two of you, sized small on purpose (no giant portions). Buy it,
rotate the meals, adjust up or down next week based on what ran out.

**Protein (the main spend):**
- Eggs: 1 dozen, plus **3 cartons of egg whites** (claras)
- Chicken: 1.5 to 2 kg (breakfast + lunch)
- Euskal Okela lean beef (steak or mince, for 1 to 2 meals): 600 to 800 g
- White fish or salmon (hake, cod): 800 g to 1 kg
- Tuna in water: 3 to 4 tins
- Ham cubes (serrano or turkey): 200 g
- High-protein yogurt: 1.5 kg (about 10 to 12 pots), plus kefir or cottage cheese if you like them

**Smart carbs (small amounts):**
- Sweet potato: 1.5 to 2 kg
- Potato: 1 kg
- Oats: 500 g (lasts ages)
- Rice: 500 g (lasts ages), or a small bag of quinoa
- Legumes: 2 tins or bags (chickpeas, lentils)
- Wholegrain bread: 1 small loaf (for Guillermo's work days)

**Fruit and fats:**
- Blueberries or other berries: 500 g (frozen is fine and cheaper)
- Walnuts / almonds: 200 g
- Extra virgin olive oil, and a couple of avocados

**Vegetables (free, buy plenty):**
- Spinach, peppers, courgette, onion, tomato, salad leaves, cucumber, green beans, cauliflower,
  garlic, lemon. Whatever is fresh and cheap that week.

*(Optional, only if you want them: creatine, omega-3, vitamin D, magnesium.)*

---

*Numbers here are careful estimates to build awareness, not a lab measurement. The scale over 2 to 3
weeks is the real test: if the weekly average is not moving, trim a little or move a little more; if
you feel wrecked, eat a bit more. Slow loss is kept loss.*
