# Tracking & Check-ins 📓 (simple, honest, kind)

You don't need to weigh every gram or count every calorie forever. But **for the first weeks,
tracking is the single most powerful tool you have** — not to judge yourself, but to *see clearly.*
Most people genuinely don't know where their calories come from until they write it down. Awareness
first.

> The scale measures one thing on one day. Your **habits** are what actually change your body. Track
> the habits, and the scale follows.

## What to track (keep it light)

1. **What you ate** — roughly. Not grams; just "eggs + toast + coffee," "big salad + tuna," "two
   beers + paella." Honesty beats precision.
2. **Weight** — ~3× a week, same conditions (morning, after the toilet, before eating). Look at the
   **weekly average**, never a single day (water weight bounces ±1–2 kg for no reason).
3. **A daily one-line check-in** — how the day went (below).
4. *(Optional)* a photo or a waist measurement every couple of weeks — sometimes the mirror and belt
   move even when the scale is being stubborn.

## The daily check-in (30 seconds)

Each evening, answer three tiny questions:

- 😊 **What went well today?** (Even small: "drank water instead of cola," "measured the oil.")
- 😬 **What was hard?** (e.g., "afternoon cravings," "couldn't resist the bar.")
- 🎯 **One thing for tomorrow?** (e.g., "carry nuts," "lighter breakfast — big dinner planned.")

This is gold. It turns autopilot into awareness, and it tells us exactly where to adjust.

> **For Guillermo (interactive version):** he can just *say it out loud* — "today I had eggs and
> coffee, a big lunch with fish and beans, and a light salad for dinner; the afternoon cravings were
> hard" — and Marten logs it. No typing, no computer skills needed. The system shows what was eaten
> and how the streak is going.

## The weekly review (5 minutes, e.g. Sunday)

Once a week, zoom out and ask:

- **Weight:** what's the weekly *average* doing vs last week?
- **Wins:** which habits am I nailing? (Keep doing them.)
- **Friction:** where did I slip most? (Build a system for it — see module 12.)
- **Adjust:** stuck for 2+ weeks? Trim ~150 kcal or add movement. Losing fast / exhausted? Eat a bit
  more.

## The big check-ins (Day 10, 30, 45)

We'll deliberately pause at a few points to talk it through — not a test, a conversation:

- **Day 10:** "How's it feeling? What's been easy, what's been hard? What should we tweak?" (Early
  course-correction so small problems don't become reasons to quit.)
- **Day 30 (halfway):** "Look how far you've come. What's working? What do we keep?"
- **Day 45:** "What are the 4–5 habits you keep for life?"

## Reading the results without drama

- **Weight isn't linear.** It zig-zags. A flat or up week inside a downward month is totally normal —
  water, salt, sleep, stress, and (for the ladies) hormones all move the number.
- **Trends beat moments.** Draw the line through a month, not a day.
- **Non-scale wins count:** better sleep, more energy on site, looser belt, clearer skin, steadier
  mood. Track those too — they often show up first.

> Be kind in the logbook. It's a **camera, not a courtroom.** We're gathering information to help you,
> not evidence to punish you. A bad day written down honestly is more useful than a perfect day that
> didn't happen.
