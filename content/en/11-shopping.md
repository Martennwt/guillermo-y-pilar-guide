# Shopping 🛒 (win the week before it starts)

Here's a quiet truth most people miss:

> **You don't fight temptation in the kitchen at 10 p.m. You beat it in the supermarket on Saturday.**

If the chips, biscuits, and cola never come into the house, you don't need willpower to resist them at
night — they're simply not there. If the fridge is full of eggs, yogurt, fish, and vegetables, the
easy choice *is* the good choice. **Win the shopping trip, win the week.**

## The golden rules of shopping

1. **Never shop hungry.** Hungry-you fills the cart with sugar and impulse buys. Eat first.
2. **Make a list and stick to it.** Decide when you're calm, buy on autopilot.
3. **Shop the edges of the store.** Fresh food (vegetables, fish, eggs, dairy) usually lives around
   the outside walls. The processed stuff lives in the middle aisles.
4. **Read one number:** "of which sugars." It'll change what you buy fast.
5. **If it shouldn't be in the house, don't buy it "for guests."** It'll be you, on the couch, at
   night. Buy treats *single-serve* and *occasionally*, not in big bags.

## The buy / don't-buy list

*(Build note: render as a two-column card — green "Fill the cart" vs red "Leave on the shelf".)*

### ✅ Fill the cart with these
- **Vegetables** — fresh and frozen (frozen is cheap, lasts, and is just as nutritious)
- **Salad** stuff — lettuce, tomato, cucumber, peppers, onion
- **Fish & seafood** — fresh, frozen, and tinned (tuna, sardines, anchovies, mackerel)
- **Eggs** — the perfect cheap protein
- **Chicken, turkey, lean cuts**
- **Beans, lentils, chickpeas** — dried or tinned
- **Plain yogurt, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese**
- **Fruit** — whatever's in season
- **Nuts** (unsalted) — in small bags, not buckets
- **Olive oil** — good quality, and an **oil spray bottle**
- **Wholegrain bread, oats, brown rice** — in sensible amounts
- **Garlic, lemon, herbs, spices, vinegar, smoked paprika** — flavor without calories
- **Water, coffee, tea, sparkling water**

### ⚠️ Leave on the shelf (or buy rarely, single-serve)
- **Sugary drinks** — cola, juice, energy drinks, sweet iced tea (the #1 swap)
- **Sweets, biscuits, pastries, cakes, chocolate bars**
- **Chips and fried/packaged snacks**
- **Processed meats** as a daily item — sausages, bacon, chorizo, hot dogs (occasional, not staple)
- **White bread and sugary breakfast cereals**
- **"Light" / low-fat products loaded with sugar** — read the label
- **Ready-meals and instant noodles**
- **Big tubs of ice cream / family bags of anything** — if it's in the house, it gets eaten

## Smart swaps (same craving, better choice)

| Instead of… | Buy… |
|---|---|
| Cola / juice | Sparkling water + a squeeze of lemon |
| Potato chips | Unsalted nuts, or homemade baked veg / popcorn |
| Sugary yogurt | Plain yogurt + fresh fruit |
| White bread | Wholegrain bread (and less of it) |
| Chocolate bar | A square or two of 85% dark chocolate |
| Ice cream tub | Frozen berries + yogurt, or single-serve |
| Sausages daily | Fresh fish, chicken, eggs (sausage as occasional treat) |

## A note for the cook (Pilar 👩‍🍳)

The shopping list is the kitchen's ammunition. With the right basics in the house — fresh fish,
vegetables, beans, eggs, good oil to *measure* — every meal you make is automatically lighter and
healthier, with **no change to the recipes you're proud of.** You're the most important teammate in
this whole plan.
