# Breakfast Options 🍳 (built around what you actually like)

Breakfast sets the tone for the whole day. A good one — with **protein and some fiber** — keeps you
full till lunch and stops the mid-morning vending-machine raid. A sugary one (pastry, sweet cereal,
juice) spikes you and leaves you hungry by 11.

We already know one thing about Guillermo: **he's not keen on vegetable juices.** Good — noted. You
don't have to drink green stuff to be healthy. Here are options for real people with real tastes.

## The breakfast formula

A great breakfast = **protein + fiber + (optional) a little healthy fat.** Hit that and almost
anything works:

- **Protein:** eggs, yogurt, cottage cheese, a little lean ham, fish, a protein shake (as a meal,
  not an add-on).
- **Fiber:** whole fruit, vegetables, whole oats, wholegrain bread.
- **Fat (small):** olive oil drizzle, a few nuts, some avocado.

## 8 breakfasts to choose from

1. **Basque classic, upgraded:** wholegrain toast with crushed **tomato + a little olive oil**, plus
   a **boiled or scrambled egg** (and a thin slice of good ham if you like). Coffee, no sugar.
2. **Yogurt bowl:** plain Greek yogurt + fresh fruit (berries, apple, pear) + a few **walnuts** +
   cinnamon. (See the honey note below.) Filling, fast, no cooking.
3. **Veggie omelette:** 2–3 eggs with peppers, onion, tomato, a little cheese. Classic, very filling,
   low-carb.
4. **Proper porridge:** whole oats cooked in milk/water, topped with fruit and nuts. (This is the
   *chewable* oats — far more filling than the shake.)
5. **Tortilla / scrambled eggs with vegetables** + a piece of fruit.
6. **Cottage cheese** + tomato + olive oil + wholegrain crackers, or + fruit for a sweeter version.
7. **Smart shake (replacement):** mostly **protein** powder + milk/water + a few berries + a small
   spoon of oats. Pair with a boiled egg or fruit so you chew something.
8. **Leftovers breakfast:** last night's fish/chicken + vegetables. Unusual? Yes. Very filling and
   protein-rich? Also yes.

## The honey & sweetener note 🍯

You don't have to give up sweetness — just keep it small and choose well:

- **Good honey is still sugar** to your body. A *small* spoon (½–1 tsp) on yogurt is fine; three big
  spoons turns a healthy breakfast into a dessert. Measure it like you measure oil.
- **Whole fruit** is the best sweetener — it brings fiber, water, and vitamins along with the
  sweetness.
- Cinnamon, vanilla, and berries make things taste sweet with almost no sugar.

## About smoothies (give them a fair second chance) 🥤

Guillermo didn't love the *vegetable* juices — totally fair, many people don't. But smoothies are a
big family, and there's almost certainly one he'd enjoy. The trick is to make them **a chewable-ish
meal, not a sugar drink:**

- Base on **plain yogurt or milk + a little fruit** (not three bananas and juice).
- Add **protein** (yogurt, a scoop of protein powder) so it's filling.
- Lean to **berries** (lower sugar) and add a spoon of oats or nuts for staying power.
- Keep it as a **breakfast replacement**, occasionally — not an extra drink on top of meals.

Why bother at all? A *well-built* smoothie is an easy way to get fruit, yogurt, and protein into a
busy morning, and people report they feel good on them — better circulation of getting real fruit and
nutrients in, support for digestion from the yogurt, and an easy hit of vegetables you'd otherwise
skip. But — per module 03 — **if liquid breakfasts leave Guillermo hungry, a plate he chews is
better.** Try one or two; keep it only if it actually fills him up and he enjoys it. No forcing.

> The rule for all of the above: **if it keeps you full till lunch and you like it, it's a good
> breakfast.** If you're hungry an hour later, add more protein or switch to something you chew.
