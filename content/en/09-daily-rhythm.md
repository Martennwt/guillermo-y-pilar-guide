# Your Daily Rhythm ⏰ (when to eat, when to stop)

*What* you eat matters most. But *when* and *how you spread it across the day* is a powerful, easy
lever — especially for managing hunger and those evening slips.

## The big idea: budget your day like money 💶

You have a calorie "budget" each day (your target from the calculator). The smartest eaters don't
spend it evenly out of habit — they **spend it where it's worth it.**

> Big dinner planned tonight — paella, wine, the whole table with family? **Great. Then keep
> breakfast and lunch light.** A small yogurt in the morning, a light lunch, and you arrive at dinner
> with budget to spend — and you actually *enjoy* it, guilt-free.

This is how normal, social, Basque life and weight loss live together. You don't skip the celebration.
You **plan around it.** Some days are front-loaded, some back-loaded. Over the week, it balances.

## A simple default rhythm

When there's no special occasion, this rhythm keeps hunger low and fat-burning on:

1. **Eat real meals, not constant snacks.** Grazing all day keeps your body running on outside fuel
   and never burning its own (remember module 02). Three solid meals — or two bigger ones — beat
   nibbling from morning to night.
2. **Make one meal the "anchor."** For Guillermo on a work day, that's often lunch — the body needs
   fuel for hard labor. Put the bigger portion where the activity is.
3. **Stop eating a few hours before bed.** Try to have your **last food ~3 hours before sleep.** Late
   eating tends to be mindless (couch + TV + snacks), and an earlier "kitchen's closed" cuts a huge
   amount of accidental calories. It also helps sleep and digestion.
4. **Close the kitchen.** After dinner, the kitchen is *closed.* Tea, water, brush your teeth — signal
   to your brain that eating is done for the day.

## The eating window (gentle, optional)

Many people find it easier to eat within a **window of about 10–12 hours** (say, first food at 8–9
a.m., last food by 7–8 p.m.) and just drink water/coffee/tea outside it. This isn't extreme fasting —
it's simply *not* eating from waking to bedtime. Benefits: fewer total calories, more hours for your
body to tap fat reserves, better sleep.

> Start gentle. Even just "no food after dinner" is a big win. You can tighten the window later if it
> feels good. Never force it on a hard-work day if you're truly hungry — fuel the work.

## A rough day on the building site (example)

- **~6:30 — Coffee** (no sugar). Light start.
- **~9:30 — Breakfast / almuerzo:** something with protein — eggs, yogurt, wholegrain bread with
  tomato and a little ham, fruit. Real food to fuel the morning.
- **~13:30–14:00 — Lunch (the anchor):** the biggest meal — fish/meat + vegetables + a portion of
  beans/potatoes/rice. This powers the afternoon.
- **~17:30 — Small snack if needed:** fruit + nuts, or a yogurt. (Skip if not hungry.)
- **~20:30 — Dinner (lighter):** salad or vegetable soup + eggs or fish. Then **kitchen closed.**
- **Evening:** water, tea, or one coffee. The couch is for resting, not grazing.

## The one habit that beats evening snacking

Evening, on the couch, in front of the TV is where most plans die — not from hunger, from **habit.**
So we replace the habit, we don't fight it:

- A big **glass of water or a herbal tea** in your hand.
- **Brush your teeth** early — a surprisingly strong "done eating" signal.
- If you truly want something: a **pre-decided** small option (a square of dark chocolate, a yogurt,
  a piece of fruit) — chosen in advance, not hunted for at 10 p.m.

> Remember: it's not willpower, it's the **system** you set up earlier. Calm, full daytime-you makes
> the rule. Tired evening-you just follows it.
