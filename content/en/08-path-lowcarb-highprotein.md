# Path 2 — Low-Carb / High-Protein 🍳 (the "get-in-shape" way)

This is the path a lot of fitness coaches reach for when someone wants to lose fat and feel a
difference **fast.** It's not magic and it doesn't break the rules from module 02 — you still need to
eat below your burn. But it stacks the deck in your favor by leaning on the two most powerful levers
for appetite and body composition: **more protein, fewer refined carbs.**

> You do **not** have to cut carbs to zero. "Lower-carb" just means fewer *refined* carbs (sugar,
> white bread, pastries, big portions of rice/pasta) and **more protein and vegetables** in their
> place.

## Why it tends to work fast

1. **Protein is the most filling food there is.** Eat more of it and you're simply less hungry — so
   the calorie deficit happens almost by itself, without willpower.
2. **Protein protects your muscle** while you lose fat. Important for someone doing hard physical
   work — you want to lose fat, not strength.
3. **Cutting refined carbs kills the spike-and-crash** that drives afternoon cravings.
4. **Quick early "water-weight" drop.** When you lower carbs, your body sheds some stored water in
   the first week. The scale moves fast early — motivating! — just know that first kilo or two is
   mostly water, and the *real* fat loss is the steady part after.

## What to eat

**Build every meal around protein + vegetables:**
- 🥩🍗🐟🥚 Meat, poultry, fish, seafood, eggs
- 🧀 Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, cheese (watch quantity)
- 🥬 Vegetables and salad — unlimited, fill the plate
- 🥑 Healthy fats in moderation: olive oil (measured), avocado, nuts
- 🫘 Some beans/lentils (they have carbs *and* protein/fiber — fine in modest amounts)

**Keep small / occasional:**
- 🍚 Rice, pasta, potatoes (smaller portions, ideally around your active days)
- 🍞 Bread (choose wholegrain, smaller amounts)
- 🍓 Fruit (whole fruit is fine; berries are lowest in sugar)

**Cut hard (the wins are biggest here):**
- 🥤 Sugary drinks and juice, 🍰 sweets and pastries, 🍟 chips and fried snacks, white sugar

## Hit your protein target

Aim for roughly **1.6–2.0 g of protein per kg of body weight** (see `calorie-calc.md`). Easy way to
think about it: **a palm-sized portion of protein at every meal**, plus protein snacks (yogurt, eggs,
a small handful of nuts, a protein shake *as a replacement*, not an add-on).

## A sample low-carb / high-protein day

- **Breakfast:** 2–3 eggs (scrambled/omelette) with vegetables and a bit of cheese; or Greek yogurt
  with berries and nuts. Coffee, no sugar.
- **Lunch:** big plate — grilled chicken or fish, large salad or cooked vegetables, drizzle of olive
  oil, small portion of beans. Fruit if you want.
- **Snack:** boiled eggs, a yogurt, or a small handful of nuts.
- **Dinner:** steak or fish or an omelette + lots of vegetables. Keep starches small, especially on
  low-activity days.

## Honest cautions

- It can feel **low-energy for the first few days** while your body adapts to using more fat for
  fuel ("keto flu" if you go very low-carb). Drink water, get enough salt, and it passes. For someone
  doing hard labor, **don't go extreme** — keep *some* smart carbs around your work days for energy.
- **Carnivore** (only meat and eggs, zero plants) is the extreme end of this path. Some people love
  it short-term, but it's restrictive, can be heavy on processed/red meat, and cuts out the fiber and
  plant nutrients that protect your heart and gut. We **don't recommend it as a default** — mention it
  only so you know where the spectrum ends.

## Mediterranean vs. Low-carb — which to pick?

| | 🫒 Mediterranean | 🍳 Low-carb / High-protein |
|---|---|---|
| Best if you… | love fish, vegetables, beans, bread, Pilar's cooking | love meat & eggs, want faster early results |
| Speed of early results | steady | often faster at first (incl. water weight) |
| Easiest to keep long-term | very easy (it's how you already eat) | easy *if* you like protein-heavy meals |
| Heart & longevity evidence | strongest of any diet | good, especially vs. sugar/refined carbs |
| The catch | watch the oil and portions | don't go extreme; keep some smart carbs for hard work |

> **There is no wrong answer.** The best diet is the one **you'll actually keep.** Many people do a
> blend: Mediterranean base + extra protein and fewer refined carbs. Pick a starting point, try it
> for 2–3 weeks, and adjust. We're building a lifestyle, not taking an exam.
