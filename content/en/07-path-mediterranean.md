# Path 1 — The Mediterranean Way 🫒 (recommended start)

This is the path we suggest starting with, for a simple reason: **it's basically how Pilar already
cooks** — Basque and Mediterranean food — just dialed in a little. No new weird ingredients, no
fighting your own kitchen. It's also the most studied diet on earth for **heart health and long
life**, and people find it easy to stick with because the food is delicious.

> The Mediterranean way isn't a "diet" you suffer. It's fish, vegetables, beans, good olive oil,
> fruit, and the occasional glass of wine — eaten in sensible amounts. The Basque kitchen is already
> halfway there.

## What it looks like (the plate)

**Eat most days — the base of everything:**
- 🥬 Vegetables and salad — lots, every meal (half the plate)
- 🐟 Fish and seafood — several times a week (the Basque coast is perfect for this)
- 🫘 Beans, lentils, chickpeas — cheap, filling, brilliant
- 🥚 Eggs
- 🫒 Olive oil — your main fat, but **measured** (see below)
- 🍅 Fruit for something sweet
- 🥜 A small handful of nuts
- 🌾 Whole grains in modest amounts (whole-grain bread, brown rice)

**A few times a week:**
- 🍗 Poultry, 🧀 some cheese and yogurt (plain)

**Occasionally, on purpose:**
- 🥩 Red meat, 🍷 wine (1 glass), 🍰 a real dessert you actually love

**Rarely / guest only:**
- Processed meat, sugary drinks, pastries, fried fast food, ultra-processed snacks

## The ONE habit that changes the Basque kitchen most: measure the oil 🫒

Olive oil is healthy — but it's also the most calorie-dense thing in the kitchen. One tablespoon is
~120 calories, and a generous "glug" into the pan can be 3–4 tablespoons (~400+ calories) you never
even taste.

You don't have to give it up. Just **control the amount**:

- Use a **spoon to measure** (1–2 tablespoons for the pan) instead of pouring straight from the
  bottle.
- Or use an **oil spray bottle** — a few sprays coat the pan with a fraction of the oil.
- **Grill, bake, steam, or stew** more, and deep-fry less. (Basque cooking already loves grilling
  fish and making stews — lean into that.)
- Build flavor with **garlic, lemon, vinegar, herbs, smoked paprika, peppers, onion** instead of
  extra oil.

This single change can quietly remove hundreds of calories a day **without changing a single dish on
the menu.** That's the kind of lever we love: no sacrifice, big result.

## A sample Mediterranean day (adjust to your calorie target)

- **Breakfast:** plain yogurt with berries, a few walnuts, cinnamon (and oats if you like) — or
  wholegrain toast with tomato, olive oil drizzle, and a boiled egg.
- **Lunch (the bigger meal):** grilled or stewed fish or chicken + a big salad or cooked vegetables +
  a portion of beans or potatoes. Fruit after.
- **Snack (if needed):** a piece of fruit + a small handful of nuts, or a yogurt.
- **Dinner (lighter):** vegetable soup or a big salad + eggs or fish. Keep it light, especially if
  the day was less active.

## Why it works for *you* specifically

- It fits the **food culture you live in** — markets full of fresh fish, vegetables, and good oil.
- Pilar's skills become a **superpower**, not an obstacle: the same beautiful dishes, slightly
  lighter.
- It's **heart-protective**, which matters for a body doing hard physical work for years to come.
- It doesn't feel like deprivation, so you actually keep it — and *kept* is the whole point.
