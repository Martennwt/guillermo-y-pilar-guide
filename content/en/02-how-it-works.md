# How Weight Actually Works 🔑

People make this complicated. It isn't. Here's the whole thing in one breath, then we'll unpack the
two or three "aha" pieces that make it click.

> **Eat less energy than your body uses → you lose weight.**
> **Eat more than it uses → you gain.**
> Everything else is detail.

## 1. Your "base number" (how much you burn)

Your body burns energy all day just existing — heart beating, breathing, staying warm, plus all your
movement and work. Add it up and you get your **daily burn** (coaches call it TDEE).

- A body that does **hard physical work**, like yours on the building site, burns **a lot**. That's
  good news: you don't need to starve. You need to be a little *below* your burn, consistently.
- We estimate your number from your height, weight, age, and activity (see the calculator). It's not
  perfect — it's a starting line we adjust.

> ⚠️ **Weekend trap:** your burn on a hard work day is much higher than on a lazy Sunday. The same
> big meal is "fine" on Tuesday and a surplus on Sunday. We'll handle this with *budgeting* (module 09).

## 2. Calories in vs. calories out (the only rule that always holds)

Think of it like a bank account:

- **Eat below your burn → you withdraw from your fat savings → you lose fat.**
- **Eat above your burn → you deposit into fat savings → you gain.**

A small daily withdrawal of about **−500 kcal** loses roughly **half a kilo a week**. Steady,
sustainable, and easy to keep.

You don't need to count every calorie forever. You need **awareness** of where the big ones are
(that's the next modules). Find the hidden ones and the math takes care of itself.

## 3. The aha-moment: metabolic flexibility 🔥

Here's the part almost nobody explains, and it changes everything.

Your body can run on two fuels: the **food you just ate**, and the **fat you've stored**. A healthy
body switches between them easily — that's called *metabolic flexibility*.

But if you eat constantly — bite, snack, bite, snack, all day — your body gets used to a steady drip
of fuel from the *outside*. It rarely has to reach into its own fat reserves. So it gets *lazy* at
burning fat.

Then the moment you *don't* eat for a few hours, your body panics a little:
*"Where's my fuel?!"* — even though you're literally carrying plenty of it as stored fat. That panic
shows up as:

- a sudden, urgent **craving**,
- feeling **weak, shaky, or irritable** ("hangry").

These feel like real emergencies. **They usually aren't.** They're the symptoms of a body that never
practiced burning its own reserves. The fix isn't to panic-eat — it's to gently teach your body to
use its fat again (eating real meals with enough protein and fiber, not grazing all day, and being
okay with a little normal hunger between meals).

## 4. Cravings ≠ hunger (impulse control is the biggest lever)

> A craving feels like hunger but usually isn't. It's a **habit and reward signal** knocking on the
> door. You're allowed to not answer.

Real hunger builds slowly and is calm ("I could eat"). A craving is sudden, specific, and loud
("I NEED chocolate NOW"). Once you can *tell them apart*, you've won half the battle — because now
you can pause.

This is the secret the best say out loud: **losing weight is rarely a willpower problem. It's a
system problem.** Willpower is weak and tired exactly when temptation is strongest (evening, couch,
stress). So we don't rely on it. We build systems:

- decide meals in advance, when you're calm and full;
- don't keep the trigger foods in the house;
- have a tiny plan for the dangerous moments (a glass of water, a handful of nuts, a 10-minute wait).

When the impulse comes, you don't need to be a hero. You just follow the plan you set up earlier.

## Putting it together

1. Know your **base number** (burn).
2. Stay a little **below it** most days (≈ −500 kcal).
3. Build **awareness** of where calories hide.
4. Eat in a way that lets your body **burn its own fat** again.
5. Beat cravings with **systems**, not willpower.
