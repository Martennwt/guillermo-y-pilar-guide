# The Cheat Sheet 📄 (the whole guide on the fridge door)

If you forget everything else, keep these few pages. This is the guide squeezed down to what you can
stick on the fridge and glance at on a hard day. Simple, blunt, and it fits on a few sheets of paper.

*(Build note: this whole module is print-first. Each block below is meant to land on its own page, so it
prints as a clean 3 to 4 page cheat sheet. Keep page breaks between the main blocks.)*

---

## Page 1: The 7 biggest levers 🎯

Not the easiest seven. The ones with the biggest payoff for the effort. Do mostly these and the rest
takes care of itself.

1. **Stop drinking your calories.** Cola, juice, sugary coffee, and most of the alcohol. This is the
   fastest, biggest win there is, and you barely feel it. One can of cola is about a third of a day's
   sugar budget, gone in two minutes.
2. **Protein and vegetables first, every meal.** Fill half the plate with vegetables, add a palm of
   protein (fish, eggs, meat, beans), then a smaller side of bread, rice, or potato. You end up full on
   fewer calories. This is the lever that makes hunger bearable.
3. **Don't keep the trigger food in the house.** You don't beat the biscuits at 10 p.m. on the couch.
   You beat them on Saturday in the shop, by not buying them. If it isn't there, you won't eat it.
4. **Close the kitchen after dinner.** Last food about 3 hours before bed, then water or tea, brush
   your teeth, done. The evening couch is where most plans quietly die. Shut that window.
5. **Move every day.** Not the gym, just movement: a walk after dinner, the stairs, parking further
   away, getting off one stop early. Steps add up to real calories and they cool down cravings.
6. **Protect your sleep.** One bad night and your hunger hormones turn up the volume the next day. Good
   sleep is not lazy. It is a weight-loss tool. Treat it like one.
7. **Measure your fats.** Oil with a spoon or a spray bottle, never a free pour. One tablespoon of
   olive oil is about 120 kcal. Great food, easy to drown a healthy meal in it without noticing.

> **One line to remember:** it is not about willpower. It is about setting things up so you barely need
> any.

## Page 2: Avoid this → Do this instead 🔁

The typical stumbling blocks, and the simple swap for each. Read the left side, do the right side.

*(Build note: render as a two-column table, red "Avoid" on the left, green "Do instead" on the right.)*

| ❌ Avoid this | ✅ Do this instead |
|---|---|
| Cola, juice, energy drinks | Sparkling water with lemon, coffee or tea with no sugar |
| A free pour of oil into the pan | Measure it: one spoon, or an oil spray (1 tbsp ≈ 120 kcal) |
| Eating in front of the TV | Eat at the table, on a plate, and skip the automatic second helping |
| A mountain of white bread or rice | Half a plate of vegetables first, a smaller side of carbs |
| Shopping hungry with no list | Eat first, shop the edges of the store, stick to the list |
| "Just one" from the big bag | Buy single-serve, and keep it out of the house |
| Snacking all day long | 2 or 3 real meals, and let the body burn its own fat in between |
| Sausages, bacon, chorizo every day | Fish, eggs, chicken. Cured meat as an occasional treat, not a staple |
| The late-night cupboard hunt | Kitchen closed, water or tea, brush your teeth, any treat pre-decided |
| A pastry breakfast | Eggs or yogurt, so you stay full until lunch |
| Sitting still all evening | A 10-minute walk after dinner |
| "I blew it, I'll start Monday" | Next meal, back to normal. One slip is not a collapse |
| Weighing daily and panicking | Weigh weekly at the same time, and judge by the trend |
| Eating because you're stressed | Name it first (see HALT), then walk, breathe, or call someone |

## Page 3: The six that quietly decide it 🔑

The big diet stuff gets all the attention. These six decide whether it actually sticks.

- **😴 Sleep.** Short sleep raises your hunger hormone (ghrelin) and your cravings the very next day.
  You wake up hungrier and reach for sugar to stay awake. Guard your sleep like part of the plan,
  because it is.
- **😤 Stress and feelings.** A lot of eating is not hunger, it is mood. When the urge hits, run a
  quick check called **HALT: am I Hungry, Angry, Lonely, or Tired?** If it is really one of those three
  feelings, food won't fix it. Walk, breathe, drink water, or call someone first.
- **🚶 Move more, all day.** You cannot out-train a fork, but daily movement still counts: a walk after
  meals, the stairs, parking further off. Aim loosely for more steps than yesterday. It burns real
  calories and quiets the urge to graze.
- **💪 Keep your muscle.** When you lose weight you want to lose fat, not muscle. Two things protect it:
  enough protein (a palm at every meal) and using your muscles a little (Guillermo gets this at work,
  Pilar can with light strength moves or carrying the shopping). Muscle keeps your metabolism up and
  keeps you strong and steady as the years go.
- **⏳ Be patient, plateaus are normal.** The scale does not drop in a straight line. Some weeks it
  sticks, then it moves again. That is not failure, it is how bodies work. Stay the course, judge by the
  month, not the morning.
- **📏 Track it right.** Don't let the daily scale mess with your head. Water, salt, and a big meal swing
  it a kilo overnight and it means nothing. Weigh once a week, same morning, same conditions. Better
  still, watch how your belt sits and how your clothes fit. That is the real progress.

> **Tiny tweaks that punch above their weight:** eat at the table with no screen, use a slightly smaller
> plate, and wait two minutes before going back for seconds. The craving for "more" usually passes.
