# Danger Zones 🛟 (eating out, parties & life on the go)

Most plans don't fail at home with a salad in front of you. They fail in **predictable situations** —
the bar with friends, the family feast, the long day away from home, the petrol-station snack. The
good news: because they're *predictable*, you can have a **system ready** for each one. No willpower
heroics — just a plan you decided in advance.

> Remember: it's not about being perfect or antisocial. You're Basque-life-proofing your goal so you
> can enjoy the paella *and* keep losing weight.

## Danger zone 1 — Eating out / restaurants 🍽️

- **Budget for it (module 09).** Know it's coming? Keep breakfast and lunch lighter, arrive with
  room to spend.
- **Decide before you arrive** roughly what you'll have, while you're calm.
- **Order protein + vegetables as the base**, then add the treat you actually want — not everything.
- **Drink water alongside alcohol**, and a glass of water *before* the meal. It paces you and fills
  you a little.
- **You don't have to clean the plate.** Portions out are huge. Eat until satisfied, not stuffed —
  leaving food is allowed (and the first few times, just deciding "I'm not finishing all of this" is
  a win).
- **Slow down.** Put the fork down between bites. Fullness signals take ~20 minutes to arrive.

## Danger zone 2 — Parties, family meals, celebrations 🎉

- **Eat a small protein snack before you go** (a yogurt, a boiled egg, a handful of nuts) so you don't
  arrive starving and attack the snack table.
- **Pick your treats on purpose.** Have the dishes you truly love, skip the ones you don't care about
  ("I'll have Pilar's dish and the good ham — I'll pass on the random crisps").
- **Stand away from the food table.** Mindless grazing happens when the bowl is at arm's reach.
- **One plate, sit down, enjoy it.** Then switch to talking, not grazing.
- **Alcohol is liquid calories** (and lowers your guard). Alternate each drink with water; decide a
  number beforehand.

## Danger zone 3 — Life on the go / away from home 🚗

This is where the famous trick lives:

> **Always carry a small pack of nuts (or fruit, or a boiled egg).** When hunger hits far from a good
> option, you're covered — and you walk past the pastry counter and petrol-station snacks without a
> fight.

- Keep **water** with you. Thirst often masquerades as hunger.
- If you must grab food out: look for the **protein + vegetable** option (a sandwich with real
  filling and salad beats a pastry; grilled beats fried).
- A planned snack in your pocket beats a desperate decision at the counter every time.

## Danger zone 4 — Evening, couch, TV 📺

The biggest one, and it's covered in module 09: the kitchen **closes** after dinner. Water or tea in
hand, brush your teeth early, and any treat is **pre-decided**, not hunted for at 10 p.m.

## The mindset that protects you: don't fall back into old patterns

One big meal out, one party, one bad day does **not** undo your progress — the same way one salad
doesn't fix a year. The danger isn't the single slip; it's letting it become *"well, I already blew
it, might as well give up."* That story is how people slide all the way back.

> **The rule:** next meal, back to normal. No guilt, no punishment, no "starting again Monday." You
> didn't fail — you lived your life and now you're back on the road. One exit doesn't mean you crash
> the car.
