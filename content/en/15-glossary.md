# Glossary 📖 (every word, in plain language)

No jargon left behind. If a word in this guide ever felt fuzzy, here it is in one plain sentence.

- **Calorie (kcal)** — a unit for the *energy* in food. Eat more energy than you burn → you store it
  as fat; eat less → you burn fat. That's the whole game.

- **Calorie balance (energy balance)** — the difference between calories in (food) and calories out
  (everything your body does). Negative = losing, positive = gaining.

- **Calorie deficit** — eating a bit *less* energy than you burn. A deficit of ~500 kcal/day loses
  about half a kilo a week.

- **BMR (basal metabolic rate)** — the energy your body uses just to stay alive at rest (heart,
  breathing, warmth). Your baseline before any movement.

- **TDEE (total daily energy expenditure)** — your *total* daily burn: BMR + all your movement and
  work. The "base number" you want to eat slightly under. Hard physical work makes it high.

- **Macronutrients ("macros")** — the three nutrients that give calories: **protein, carbohydrates,
  fat.**

- **Protein** — the body's building blocks (eggs, fish, meat, yogurt, beans). The **most filling**
  macro, and it protects your muscle while you lose fat.

- **Carbohydrates (carbs)** — the body's quick energy (bread, rice, pasta, potatoes, sugar, fruit).
  Not evil — but refined/sugary carbs are easy to overeat and cause spike-and-crash hunger.

- **Fat (dietary)** — the most calorie-dense macro (9 kcal/g). Healthy fats (olive oil, fish, nuts,
  avocado) are great in *measured* amounts; industrial/trans fats are the harmful kind.

- **Fiber** — the part of plants you don't fully digest (vegetables, beans, whole grains, fruit).
  Adds bulk, slows digestion, keeps you full and your gut happy.

- **Energy density** — how many calories are packed into each bite. Vegetables and soups = low (eat
  lots); oil, chocolate, chips = high (small amounts add up fast).

- **Metabolic flexibility** — your body's ability to switch between burning food and burning stored
  fat. Constant snacking makes it lazy at burning fat; real meals + some gaps wake it back up.

- **Free sugars** — sugar *added* to food, plus the sugar in honey, syrups, and fruit juice. The WHO
  says keep it under ~50 g/day (ideally ~25 g). Note: the sugar *inside whole fruit* doesn't count
  here, because the fiber comes with it.

- **Ultra-processed food (UPF)** — industrial products made from cheap ingredients, additives,
  colors, and flavors you couldn't make at home (sweets, chips, sugary drinks, many ready-meals).
  Linked to overeating and worse health.

- **Trans fats** — harmful industrial fats (in some margarines, fried fast food, packaged baked
  goods, "partially hydrogenated oils"). They raise heart-disease risk; aim for zero.

- **Glycemic spike (and crash)** — when fast carbs/sugar shoot your blood sugar up quickly, it often
  drops just as fast soon after — bringing on a craving. Protein, fiber, and fat smooth it out.

- **Satiety** — the feeling of being full and satisfied. Driven by protein, fiber, water/volume, and
  chewing.

- **Mediterranean diet** — an eating pattern based on vegetables, fish, beans, fruit, olive oil, and
  whole grains, with little processed food. The most evidence-backed diet for heart health and
  longevity.

- **Low-carb / high-protein** — an approach that lowers refined carbs and raises protein for strong
  appetite control and fast early results. You don't need *zero* carbs.

- **Carnivore diet** — the extreme end of low-carb: only meat and animal foods, no plants. Very
  restrictive; not recommended as a default (cuts fiber and plant nutrients).

- **Intermittent fasting / eating window** — eating within a set window each day (e.g. 8 a.m.–8 p.m.)
  and only drinking water/coffee/tea outside it. Helps cut total calories and gives the body time to
  burn fat. We use a *gentle* version.

- **Volume eating** — filling up on low-energy-density foods (vegetables, salad, soup, lean protein)
  so you eat a big, satisfying amount for few calories.

- **NEAT (non-exercise activity)** — all the calories you burn moving around outside of "exercise":
  walking, fidgeting, working, stairs. For Guillermo, his job is a huge NEAT source.

- **Maintenance** — eating roughly at your TDEE so your weight holds steady. Where you'll settle once
  you've reached your goal — the "keep the good grade" phase.
