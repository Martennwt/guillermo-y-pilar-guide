# Extra Tips 💡 (the slightly-more-medical bonus page)

A few "pro" extras that make everything else work better. Nothing here is required — but each one
gives you an edge, and a couple get nicely into the science of *why*.

---

## 🧪 The hunger hormone: Ghrelin (and why protein is your friend)

Here's a genuinely useful piece of biology. Your hunger isn't just "willpower" — it's partly run by
**hormones**, and you can influence them with what you eat.

- **Ghrelin** is the body's main **"hunger hormone."** Your stomach releases it when it's empty —
  ghrelin goes *up* and you feel hungry. After you eat, ghrelin goes *down* and the hunger quiets.
- **The key fact:** **protein lowers ghrelin more than carbs or fat do** — and it keeps it down
  longer. A high-protein breakfast suppresses ghrelin more effectively than a high-carb one.
- At the same time, protein **boosts your "I'm full" hormones** (called **GLP-1 and PYY**), which
  tell your brain to stop eating.

So when we say *"lead every meal with protein,"* this is the machinery behind it:

> **More protein → less ghrelin (less hunger) + more fullness hormones → fewer cravings, naturally.**

That's why a protein breakfast (eggs, yogurt) keeps you full till lunch, while a sugary one
(pastry, sweet cereal) leaves you raiding the cupboard by 11. You're not weak — you're just being
pushed around by hormones. Eat in a way that puts *them* on your side.

*(There's also **leptin**, the longer-term "I have enough energy stored" hormone, and **insulin**,
which manages blood sugar. You don't need to memorize them — just know: real food, enough protein,
and good sleep keep this whole hormone system calm and on your team.)*

## 🎯 Preparation beats willpower (say it again)

The single most important sentence in this whole guide, worth repeating:

> **It's not about willpower. It's about preparation.**

Willpower is weakest exactly when you need it most — tired, stressed, evening, hungry. So we don't
rely on it. We **prepare**, while calm and full, so the hard moment already has an answer:

- Meals decided in advance.
- Good food in the house, trigger food not in the house.
- Nuts in your pocket, water in your bag.
- A pre-decided treat instead of a 10 p.m. cupboard hunt.

When the craving hits, you don't fight it with grit — you just **follow the plan you already made.**

---

## ⏸️ How to beat a craving in the moment

When a craving comes (and it will), it feels urgent. It isn't. Try this:

1. **Name it:** "This is a craving, not real hunger." (Real hunger is calm and slow; cravings are
   sudden and specific.)
2. **Drink a glass of water** and wait **10 minutes.** Most cravings pass on their own — they come in
   waves.
3. **Do something with your hands** — go for a short walk, step outside, make tea, brush your teeth.
4. **If you still want it after 10 minutes,** have a small, pre-decided portion — sitting down,
   slowly, enjoying it. No guilt.

You're not at war with yourself. You're just **outlasting a wave** that always breaks.

## ❤️ Remember your WHY

When motivation dips — and some days it will — don't try to think your way back with logic. Go back
to your **feeling.**

> **Get the note out.** The one from the fridge — your real reason for starting. Read it. *"Why did I
> actually begin this?"*

Maybe it's your grandchildren. Your heart. Feeling strong on the scaffolding at 60. Looking good for
your family. Whatever you wrote — that's the engine. Re-reading it for 30 seconds restarts it.

On a tough evening, that little piece of paper beats any amount of willpower.

## A few more quick wins

- **Sleep.** Bad sleep raises ghrelin and cravings the next day — make sleep a weight-loss tool, not
  an afterthought.
- **Protein first on the plate.** Literally eat the protein and vegetables *before* the bread/rice —
  you fill up on the good stuff first.
- **Slow down.** Fullness signals take ~20 minutes. Put the fork down between bites.
- **A walk after dinner** helps blood sugar and curbs the evening grazing urge.
- **Don't drink your calories.** Water, coffee, tea. Liquids don't fill you up (module 03).
