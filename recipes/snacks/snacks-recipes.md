# Snacks & Treats 🥜🍫 (planned, not raided)

Snacks aren't the enemy — *unplanned* snacks are. Keep these pre-decided and portioned. Lead with
protein or fiber so they actually hold you over.

---

## Savory / protein snacks
- **Boiled eggs** (make a few in advance) — ~70 kcal each, pure protein. The perfect "in your pocket"
  snack.
- **Small handful of unsalted nuts** (~25–30 g) — ~170 kcal. Carry them for life-on-the-go (module 12).
- **Greek yogurt** (plain) + cinnamon — ~120 kcal, high protein.
- **Cottage cheese** + tomato or cucumber — light and filling.
- **A tin of sardines/mackerel** on a couple of wholegrain crackers — omega-3 + protein.
- **Veg sticks** (carrot, pepper, cucumber) — near-free calories, good crunch.

## Sweet-tooth-friendly (the smart treat)
- **Whole fruit** — apple, pear, orange, berries. Nature's dessert, with fiber.
- **Greek yogurt + berries + cinnamon** (+ ½ tsp honey if needed) — feels like dessert.
- **A square or two of 85% dark chocolate** — real treat, low sugar, easy to stop at two.
- **Frozen berries + a spoon of yogurt** — like ice cream, a fraction of the sugar.
- **Baked apple with cinnamon** — warm, sweet, almost no added sugar.

## The "pre-decided evening treat"
Pick ONE in advance for couch time, instead of hunting the cupboard at 10 p.m.:
- a yogurt with berries, OR
- a piece of fruit, OR
- two squares of dark chocolate.
Have it sitting down, slowly, enjoy it — then the kitchen's closed.

## Carry-kit for being out
Keep these stashed in bag/car/jacket so hunger never forces a bad choice:
- a small bag of nuts
- a piece of fruit
- a boiled egg (for shorter trips)
- a bottle of water (thirst often masquerades as hunger)

> Rule of thumb: a snack should have **protein or fiber**, fit in one hand, and be **decided before
> you're hungry.** If it ticks those boxes, it's helping you, not hurting you.
