# Myth Quiz — "What do you think?"

A short, playful true/false quiz to spark **aha-moments** before we explain anything. People learn
far better when they first commit to a guess and *then* discover the answer. Keep it light and a
little funny — no one fails this quiz, everyone learns something.

In the interactive site: tap "True" or "False", then the card flips to reveal the answer + a
one-line explanation. On paper: circle your guess, answers on the next page.

---

### 1. "Healthy foods can't make you gain weight — you can eat as much as you want."
**FALSE.** Olive oil, nuts, cheese, and avocado are healthy *and* very calorie-dense. Too much of
anything above your needs is stored as fat. Quality matters — but so does quantity.
→ See module 02 (How it works) & 04 (Volume).

### 2. "A big plate of salad with eggs and tuna can have fewer calories than one small chocolate bar."
**TRUE.** A huge satisfying salad can be ~350 kcal; a Snickers is ~250 kcal and gone in 6 bites.
Volume + chewing keep you full for hours; the candy bar doesn't.
→ See module 04 (Volume & satiety).

### 3. "An oat-flour protein shake is one of the best things for losing weight."
**IT DEPENDS — mostly FALSE for fat loss.** It's a lot of liquid carbohydrate calories you drink
in 30 seconds without chewing. It can spike, then leave you hungry again soon. Fine as an
occasional tool; not a weight-loss hero.
→ See module 03 (The shake truth).

### 4. "Cravings mean your body truly needs food right now."
**FALSE.** A craving is often a *habit and reward signal*, not real hunger. If you only ever eat,
eat, eat, your body forgets how to burn its own fat reserves — so skipping a snack feels like an
emergency when it isn't.
→ See module 02 (Metabolic flexibility & impulse control).

### 5. "Sugar is roughly as 'pull-you-back' rewarding to the brain as some addictive drugs."
**TRUE (in animal studies).** In a famous 2007 experiment, rats chose sweet water over cocaine.
Sugar strongly activates the brain's reward system — which is why "just one bite" is so hard.
→ See module 05 (Sugar & ultra-processed).

### 6. "To lose weight you have to cut carbs to zero."
**FALSE.** You don't need zero carbs — you need to be **below your calorie maintenance**. Lower
carbs and higher protein *help* with fullness, but you don't have to ban bread forever.
→ See module 02 & 08.

### 7. "Losing 3 kg fast in two weeks means the diet is working."
**FALSE (careful).** Fast loss is mostly water and easy to regain. The real skill isn't *getting*
the good grade once — it's *keeping* it. Slow and steady wins.
→ See mindset nuggets.

### 8. "Where you eat out and what you keep in the cupboard barely affects your weight."
**FALSE.** Most slips happen in predictable places (couch + TV, bar with friends, the snack
cupboard). Fix the environment and you barely need willpower.
→ See module 11 (Shopping) & 12 (Danger zones).

---

**Score is irrelevant.** The point is the surprise — and that every "wrong" answer is a door into
the guide.
