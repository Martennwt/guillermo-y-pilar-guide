# Onboarding / Intake Questions — "Meet them where they are"

The single biggest predictor of success is not the meal plan — it's a **strong personal "why"**
and a clear picture of the **current reality** (Ist-Zustand). These questions do both. They are
written to be asked gently, conversationally, with curiosity and zero judgment (the way the best
coaches do it). In the interactive site, these become a short, friendly intake flow; on paper,
they are a one-page worksheet.

> Coaching principle: when people design their own plan, with their own life in mind, they follow
> through far more often. We ask, we listen, we adapt — we don't lecture.

---

## Part 1 — The big WHY (motivation)

1. **Why do you want to lose weight? Give me 5 reasons.** (Push past "to feel better" — keep going.)
2. **Imagine it's one year from now and you reached and kept a healthy weight. How does your life
   look and feel different?** (Energy, sleep, knees/back, playing with grandchildren, clothes,
   confidence, health checkups…)
3. **What is the ONE reason that matters most to you?** (This becomes your anchor.)
4. On a scale of 1–10, **how important** is this to you right now? And **how confident** are you
   that you can do it? (If confidence is low, we start smaller.)

## Part 2 — The current reality (Ist-Zustand)

5. **Walk me through a normal day of eating** — from waking up to going to bed. Be honest, this is
   just for us. (Breakfast, lunch, dinner, drinks, snacks, second helpings.)
6. **What do you drink during the day?** (Water, coffee with sugar, soda, juice, wine, beer…)
7. **How is the food cooked at home?** (Oil amount, frying vs. grilling vs. stewing.)
8. **How many days a week do you do physical work or exercise, and how hard?**
9. **How well do you sleep, and how is your stress?** (Both quietly drive hunger and cravings.)

## Part 3 — The temptations (where it actually breaks down)

10. **When and where do you usually overeat or go off track?** (Evening on the couch? Weekends?
    Bar with friends? Work breaks? Sweets after dinner?)
11. **What are your favorite "trigger" foods** — the ones that are hard to stop eating?
12. **What does hunger feel like for you?** Do you ever eat when you're actually bored, tired,
    or stressed rather than hungry?

## Part 4 — History & preferences

13. **Have you tried to lose weight before? What happened?** What worked, what felt awful, why did
    it stop? (No shame — we learn from it.)
14. **What foods do you love and would never want to give up?** (We build around these, not against.)
15. **What foods do you dislike?** (We already know: Guillermo isn't keen on vegetable juices — noted.)
16. **Any allergies, intolerances, or medical conditions / medications we should know about?**
17. **Who shops and who cooks?** (Pilar cooks — so she's a key teammate, not a bystander.)

## Part 5 — The commitment

18. **Could you set aside 30 minutes — TV off — to sit and write your real "why"?** (We'll give a
    worksheet. The desire has to be real, or the whole thing collapses at the first temptation.)
19. **What is one small thing you'd be willing to change starting tomorrow?** (Their idea, not ours.)

---

### How to use the answers

- Q1–Q4 → fill the **"My Why" anchor** (printed on the cover of their plan, on the fridge).
- Q5–Q9 → fill `guillermo-profile.md` / `pilar-profile.md` (the baseline we measure against).
- Q10–Q12 → design **"danger zone" systems** (see `content/en/12-danger-zones.md`).
- Q13–Q17 → shape which **path** (Mediterranean vs low-carb) and which **recipes** we start with.
